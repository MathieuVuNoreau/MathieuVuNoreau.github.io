Introduction

For my final Co-op placement, I spent my time out in British Columbia working at Electronic Arts in Burnaby, BC.
This placement was my first experience working at a large corporation and has given me a large insight on the gaming industry, corporate environment, and my future in terms of career. My past experience for coops has been spent working for fairly small companies (8-15 people), this jump to one of the largest gaming companies in the world wax a surprise to say the least.
The following report will dive into my experience at EA, and my exposure to new roles as a developer.

Employer Information

Electronic Arts (EA) is a video game development and publishing company. They have recently developed and published titles such as FIFA 19, Madden 19, and Battlefield V, all of which are available on PC, PS4, and Xbox.
Due to the size and scope of EA’s products there are many teams within the organizations, teams such as the EAX team, NHL team, Frostbite team, FIFA FUT team, DRE team and many others all diving into different areas of computing science. Within the organization you may be exposed to almost all aspects of programming, whether it be software development, web development, game development, AR, AI, big data, databases, UI/UX design.
Currently, EA has 37 offices worldwide all developing, producing, managing or supporting numerous games ranging from A Way Out to SSX.

Learning Outcome

Jenkins

My first four months at EA were spent learning existing infrastructure while learning how to use various software to operate the infrastructure. One of the most useful tools ive learned at my time at EA was Jenkins. Jenkins is an automation server/service. It helps to automate the non-human part of the software development process, with continuous integration and facilitating technical aspects of continuous delivery.

AWS

While learning the Infrastructure of EA, one of the tools which is highly relied upon by the organization is Amazon Web Services. Amazon Web Services is comprised more than 90 services spanning a wide range including computing, storage, networking, database, analytics, application services, deployment, management, mobile, developer tools, and tools for the Internet of Things. The core infrastructure tools used at EA from AWS are EC2 Instances(computing), S3 buckets (storage), Elastic Search (analytics), and AWS CloudWatch (Management). I was able to utilize all these products within AWS in order to understand and contribute to the existing EA infrastructure.

Docker

On the tail end of the last 4 months I was able to explore some new(ish) technologies within the DevOps/SRE world. Throughout the DevOps/SRE industry Docker is the front runner for operating system level virtualization, also known as “containerization”. I was able to utilize docker during my time at EA when I was given the opportunity to build my own internal application used to create, edit and destroy development environments.

React.Js

For the last 4 months at EA I was able to use my existing knowledge of infrastructure and development to develop an internal tool for developers within the EAX team to use. I was able to utilize my existing front-end skills and explore React.Js for the first time. To be put simply, React.Js is a JavaScript library for building user interfaces. It is often used as a base in the development of single-page or mobile applications.

Job Description

At Electronic Arts I was given the role of Associate Developer Co-op (SRE) within the EAX Operations/SRE team.
EAX is a high performing team within EA that manages a number of digital distribution and subscription products including EA Access. The EA Access Hub is a digital distribution application from Electronic Arts that allows users to purchase subscriptions and games specifically for console platforms. Users can stay connected to everything EA Access, take their pick of all the great games in The Vault and play as much as they want. In addition, they can put upcoming EA games to the test before the release date with exclusive Play First Trials.
The Operations/SRE team incorporates aspects of software engineering and applies that to IT operations problems. Our main goal was to support other teams within EAX within a Networking/DevOps/SRE context. This included automating CI/CD pipelines, creating new development environments, solving networking problems, etc.
The most interesting part of my work term at EA was the exposure to a completely different field of computing. I had never been exposed to a DevOps/SRE role which had brought on some new challenges. With the new knowledge of networking, CI/CD, and containerization I believe I am a better-rounded developer and am able to take on most/any web development challenges.

Conclusions

Overall, I thoroughly enjoyed my time at EA. The opportunity to work with such highly skilled individuals within multi-national organization has really helped me develop my skills as a young professional beginning their career in computer science.
